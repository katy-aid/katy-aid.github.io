# Katy Aid

***Welcome to Katy!***

## About

Katy Aid is a **community resource hub** that highlights resources available to residents within the community of Katy. Ranging from **ethnic markets** and **residences** to **medical help** and **immigration assistance**, Katy Aid contains a grand collection of resources, showcasing some of the best that Katy has to offer.

Katy Aid was originally created as a submission for the **Webmaster** event of the [Technology Student Association](https://tsaweb.org), a national organization that hosts STEAM-related competitions. This was an entry for the 2026 [Gulf Coast Regional Conference](https://www.gulfcoasttsa.org) of [Texas TSA](https://texastsa.org).

## How to Navigate

The primary method of navigating Katy Aid is by using the **sitemap** at the bottom of the [home](https://katy-aid.github.io) page.

**If you're on a computer:**

- Click on any green area of your screen and drag your mouse to move around

- Use the `+` and `-` signs located at the top left of your screen to zoom in and out, respectively

**If you're on a mobile device:**

- Tap and hold any green area of your screen to move around by dragging one finger

- Using two fingers, slide your finger away from or toward each other to zoom in and out, respectively

When you find a category that you want to explore, simply click on its icon to open the popup with more details, and click the `View Resources` button. If you ever want to return to the home page, you can always click the map icon at the top left corner of your screen.

## Behind the Website

Katy Aid was created with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. The JS libraries that were utilized were [Leaflet](https://leafletjs.com) for the sitemap and [jQuery](https://jquery.com) for DOM manipulation.

### Struggles and Lessons

For our [TSA](https://tsaweb.org) submission, we had to create an interactive directory of community resources. When we began the process of developing our website, we struggled to figure out how to present the resources we had researched.

After initially having all of our resources in a bullet point list on a Google Doc, we eventually settled on creating a map-like interface to present the different categories of resources on our website. We originally made the sitemap our entire home page, but we later decided to embed the sitemap at the bottom of the page to allow us to include more features and information on the home page.

## Local Development

### Prerequisites

- A modern web browser

- A code editor

### How to Run

1. Open the terminal

2. Clone this repository with Git:

    ```bash
    git clone https://github.com/katy-aid/katy-aid.github.io.git
    cd katy-aid.github.io
    ```
3. Open your file explorer

4. Navigate to the `katy-aid.github.io` directory

5. Open `index.html` in your browser
